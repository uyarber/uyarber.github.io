An Abbreviated History of Catfishing

Catfishing has become a common practice in the internet age. Catfish is defined as taking on either a fictional persona or stealing another person’s identity online– pretending to be someone else on social media. The goal of catfishing could be financial, but often it is thought of as a crime of passion, a method of manipulation or wish fulfillment.

The original term catfish originated from the 1913 novel The Catfish about a man who is consumed by fantasy and obsession, but was popularized by the 2010 American documentary by the same name, about a man Nev Schulman who is a victim of an internet imposter. The film was the introduction to public awareness, and the impetus for the 2012 popular spinoff MTV show that Schulman continues to host. The show investigates possible incidents of catfishing nationally.
